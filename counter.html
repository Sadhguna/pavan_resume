Person Counter and Appliance Control

Person Counter and
Appliance Control

project description

This is a power conservation project besides it will keep the track of number of people who are entering and who are leaving. This prototype is kept at any entrance of a room so, that it can keep the track of count of people who are entering and leaving the room through that entrance.Not only the count but also controls the appliances of the room according to the count. when the count is above zero it switch on the appliances and when the count is zero it will off the appliances of the room(particularly the appliances which are connected to the prototype).

Electrons in the semiconductor recombine with electron holes, releasing energy in the form of photons (Energy packets). The color of the light (corresponding to the energy of the photons) is determined by the energy required for electrons to cross the band gap of the semiconductor.[5] White light is obtained by using multiple semiconductors or a layer of light-emitting phosphor on the semiconductor device.

code

#include
LiquidCrystal lcd(2,3,7,6,5,4);
#define in 8
#define out 9
#define led 10
int count=0;
void setup()
{
lcd.begin(16,2);
lcd.print("Visitor Counter");
delay(2000);
pinMode(in, INPUT);
pinMode(out, INPUT);
pinMode(led, OUTPUT);
lcd.clear();
lcd.print("Person In Room:");
lcd.setCursor(0,1);
lcd.print(count);
}
void loop()
{
int in_value = digitalRead(in);
int out_value = digitalRead(out);
if(in_value == LOW)
{
count++;
lcd.clear();
lcd.print("Person In Room:");
lcd.setCursor(0,1);
lcd.print(count);
delay(1000);
}
if(out_value == LOW)
{
count--;
lcd.clear();
lcd.print("Person In Room:");
lcd.setCursor(0,1);
lcd.print(count);
delay(1000);
}
if(count==0)
{
lcd.clear();
digitalWrite(led, LOW);
lcd.clear();
lcd.print("Nobody In Room");
lcd.setCursor(0,1);
lcd.print("Light is Off");
delay(200);
}
else
{
digitalWrite(led, HIGH);
}
}

working

Here in this video consider my hand as a person, whenever a person enters the count increases and it is displayed on the lcd along with that the led beside that is automatically turns on. When the count equals to zero the led will be turned off.
IR sensor sends the measured values to the processor(arduino), according to these values it counts people. As you can see I have placed two IR sensors in the prototype, one to detect number of people who are entering and other is to detect the number of people who are leaving. When one sensor detects something then until few seconds the other sensor won't measure anything, this helps to avoid the problem of both measuring. If both measures then what happens is one will increase the count and other will decrease the count at a time as they are places one after other, This will never increase the count. In this manner the variable count is incremented and decremented accordingly. When ever this count will be zero, then the appliances which are connected to processor will loose their power supply so, they will be automatically turnedoff. when ever this count increases they will get their power supply back and they will be turned on.
